Test Automation Maturity Model

A structured framework for assessing and improving your test automation practices across 5 key dimensions. From ad hoc to industry-leading.

Assess Your Level → Explore Dimensions

Why a Maturity Model?

Most teams know they need to improve their testing, but struggle to prioritise. Our maturity model gives you a clear picture of where you stand across 5 dimensions and a structured path forward. So you invest effort where it matters most.

The Scale

5 Maturity Levels

Each dimension is scored from Level 1 (Initial) to Level 5 (Optimising)

1

Initial

Testing is ad hoc and reactive. No formal processes, little to no automation, and quality is an afterthought.

  • No test strategy
  • Manual testing only
  • No CI/CD integration
  • Verbal defect reporting
2

Managed

Some processes exist but are inconsistently applied. Basic automation covers critical paths but is fragile.

  • Basic test plans
  • 10-30% automation
  • Flaky tests common
  • Spreadsheet tracking
3

Defined

Standardised processes and frameworks are in place. Automation is reliable and integrated into CI. Quality is part of the SDLC.

  • Documented strategy
  • 30-60% automation
  • CI/CD quality gates
  • Test management tools
4

Measured

Metrics drive decisions. Pipelines are optimised, coverage is comprehensive, and the team continuously improves based on data.

  • Risk-based strategy
  • 60-85% automation
  • Multi-stage pipelines
  • Real-time dashboards
5

Optimising

Industry-leading practices with AI-assisted testing, self-healing tests, predictive analytics, and a culture of continuous innovation.

  • Adaptive strategy
  • 85%+ automation
  • Self-healing tests
  • Predictive analytics
Assessment Areas

5 Assessment Dimensions

Each dimension captures a critical aspect of test automation maturity

Test Process & Governance

How structured and repeatable are your testing activities?

  • Test case management approach
  • Test planning and strategy maturity
  • Defect management practices
  • Process review and improvement cadence

Automation Coverage & Effectiveness

What proportion of tests are automated, and how reliable are they?

  • Regression automation percentage
  • Test reliability and flakiness management
  • Framework architecture quality
  • Test maintenance approach

Tooling & Infrastructure

How well integrated are your tools, CI/CD pipelines, and test environments?

  • CI/CD pipeline integration depth
  • Test environment management
  • Tool ecosystem breadth
  • Test data management practices

Reporting & Observability

How effectively do you measure, report, and act on quality metrics?

  • Test result reporting maturity
  • Coverage and quality metrics tracking
  • Requirements traceability
  • Root cause analysis speed

Team Skills & Culture

How does your team approach quality ownership, skills development, and collaboration?

  • Quality ownership model
  • Team automation skill level
  • Knowledge sharing practices
  • Shift-left adoption maturity
